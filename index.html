Vidyard

My first Co-Op

Let me start by saying that the people at Vidyard are all amazing individuals! Everyone there was extremely welcoming and helpful in every way.

Introduction

This will be an explination of my first Co-Op work term at Vidyard in Kitchener, Ontario. This was my first Co-Op work term and it helped me gain a greater understanding on how software companies function and how to work as a team. This post should give you insight on what i learned and how I grew as a developer over the 4 months I was at Vidyard.

Employer Info

Vidyard is a Marketing software company. Their goal is to help buisnesses succeed through the power of video. They created their own software the allows sharing videos and tracking video analytics extremely easy and have integrated the software with many other companies. They even have their own video making chrome extension. They pride them selves on having the best customer service/experience a company can offer. The company has been around for about 7 years. Vidyard is partnered with many big companies such as HubSpot, Pardot, Salesforce, and many others.

Job Description

I was hired onto the Technical Support team which was part of customer experience. I was tasked with learning the software vidyard was selling and assisting customers with any techinical issues they may have.

I often reported bugs that customers were experiencing through JIRA. I was also constantly monitoring Zendesk, a ticketing system that allowed users to report their issues to my team and allowed us to help guide the users through their concerns. Later in the Co-Op, I helped customize simple sharing pages with HTML/CSS and ran a couple ruby scripts to make mass changes to users accounts.

Once I got used to my new duties, I started taking calls with customers over the phone and Zoom to help solve their issues faster and more efficiently. I had learned my way around Salesforce, HubSpot, Pardot, JIRA, Zendesk, and many other softwares.

Goals

My main goals when starting my first Co-Op job were to learn how to work better as a team, learn better troubleshooting steps, learn how to communicate more effectively with others, learn a multitude of new software, and above all else, I made sure to show that I am a dedicated and hard worker. These goals are not just the goals for my first work term but the goals that I plan on improving throught my entier career as a software developer.

Part of working better as a team is also communicating more effectively. I leanred how to do both at the same time. I often wrote reports and update knowledge articles to improve the resources that not only our team would end up using but also our clients. These articles had to be clear and concice so that our clients were not confused. I also made sure to ask if anyone needed help when running low on work to also inform my team of potential problems that users have started reporting.

I learned how to more effectivly troubleshoot issues while working at Vidyard. I did this by asking questions and gathering as much information as possible and starting at the absolute beginning of the issue until I rooted out the problem. This took time and I asked many questions, not only did I ask questions of our clients but also to my co-workers. I learned that the solution to any problem is in the details of the situation and to learn as much as possible with quick and concice questions when trying to test and debug software.

I believe I made great progress in learning new widely used software. I leanred how to use JIRA effectivly which I know will be important to know in my future careers.

Conclusion

Vidyard was my first Co-Op and my time there was invaluable. I gained so many new experiences and all of them had a lesson in side for me. The people were amazing and the job was very insightful on how a software company operates. I believe I have come out of my position a stronger worker and a more valuable team member. I could not have picked a better place as my first Co-Op!
